# Systems Engineering Foundations
A disciplined approach for the successful realization, operation, and retirement of systems, balancing stakeholder needs, technical performance, cost, schedule, and risk.
### Core Principles
- Systems thinking, Lifecycle orientation, Interdisciplinary, Requirements driven, Risk aware, Decision based.
### Key Concepts
- System: Interacting elements to deliver value
- System boundary: Defines scope + interfaces
- Context: External systems & constraints
- Emergence: System behavior from interactions
- System of Systems (SoS): Operational independence, Managerial independence, Evolutionary development, Emergent capability
## ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288 Life Cycle
- Life Cycle Stages: Concept, Development, Production, Utilization, Support, Retirement
- Lifecycle ≠ linear → iterative & recursive
- Common Life Cycle Models: Waterfall → Sequential; V-model → verification & validation mapping; Incremental → Risk reduction; Spriral → Risk-driven; Agile → Adaptability
## Stakeholder Needs & Requirements
- Stakeholder Needs: Expressed in user language; Capture what and why, not how
- - Inputs: interviews, workshops, scenarios
- CONOPS (Very testable)
- - Describes how the system will be used
- - Operational scenarios
- - Interfaces, environment, users
- - Bridges users → engineers
Good Requirement Characteristics:	Necessary, Unambiguous, Verifiable, Feasible, Singular, Traceable
❌ Avoid: “optimize”, “user-friendly”, “fast”
## System Requirements and Architecture
- Requirement Types: Functional, Performance, Interface, Constraint (cost, schedule, regulation)
- Traceability:	Needs → System reqs → Subsystem reqs and Req → Design → Verification
If it’s not traceable, it’s a risk.
- Architecture: Logical- What the system does, and Physical- What it’s made of
- Allocation: Mapping requirements → functions → physical elements
- Design Trade Studies:	Alternatives, Evaluation criteria, Weighted decision rationale
## Verification, Validation & Transition (IVVT)
- Verification Methods:	Test (highest confidence), Demonstration, Analysis, Inspection
- Integration: Planned sequence, Interface focused, Reduces risk early
- Transition: Delivery to users, Training, Documentation, Deployment readiness
Verification - built right and validation - right system
## Technical Management
- Risk Management: Risk = Condition + Consequence (+ Probability)
- Configuration Management (CM): Protects technical baselines - Identifies, Controls, Status accounting, Audits
Risk → potential future event and Issue → already occuring
- Interface Management:	Defines internal & external interfaces, Critical for integration success
- Technical Performance Measures (TPMs): Quantitative tracking of design health, Leading indicators are more valuable than lagging
- - Example: Weight growth (leading), Failures in field (lagging)
## Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE)
- MBSE Definition: Application of models as the primary means of systems engineering information exchange.
- - Benefits: Single source of truth, Consistency, Traceability, Early analysis
- SysML Used For - Requirements, Structures, Behavior, Parametrics
⚠ Risks: Tool over focus, Poor modeling discipline
## Specialty Engineering (Know at High Level)
- Reliability → uptime, failure rates
- Maintainability → repair time
- Safety → hazard identification & mitigation
- Human Systems Integration (HSI) → usability, workload
- Cybersecurity → confidentiality, integrity, availability
- Supportability → logistics, sustainment
✅ Integrated early — not bolted on
